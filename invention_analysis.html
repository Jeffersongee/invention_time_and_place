Alignment by Category

For each invention, we ask: given the technology available at the earliest plausible date, where could it have been invented? We then compare that to where it actually was invented. ALIGNED means the actual location was among the plausible set. MISMATCH means history placed the invention somewhere that wasn't the most likely set of locations — a geographic counterfactual.

23 of the 190 inventions are mismatches — 12%. The cards below detail each case.

1804
General anaesthetic
Actual locationKii Province, Japan
Plausible locationsChina (Han dynasty medical centers); Japan (Kampo tradition; post-Tang transmission); India (Ayurvedic centers — Taxila; Kashi); Hellenistic/Roman Mediterranean (Alexandria; Rome); Persian/Islamic medical centers (Gondishapur; Baghdad; Cordoba)
The actual location (early 19th-century Japan) is much later than the earliest plausible window. Equivalent botanical pharmacopoeias existed in Han China, Ayurvedic India, and Greco-Roman medicine — any of which had the prerequisites for a working surgical anaesthetic preparation centuries earlier.

1844
